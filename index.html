Heywork: Building Trust in a Low-Trust Market

Bootstrapped from Idea to 400 Users with Zero Marketing Budget.

View Works
62% Acquisition from 1 Viral Video
₹0 Acquisition Cost (Organic)
90% Job Completion Rate

The Situation

The blue-collar hiring market in India is broken. Middlemen steal 10-15% of wages, and existing platforms are flooded with scams. The problem wasn't "Jobs"—it was "Trust." We had limited funds. Most capital went into tech (app development). I had exactly ₹0 for marketing.

The "Pain-Point" Strategy

Since paid ads were impossible, I had to rely on organic reach. I realized generic "Hire Now" posts were invisible. I shifted to a "Problem-First" strategy.

I created a hyper-realistic AI video depicting the life of a Kerala restaurant owner facing labor shortages—the headache of managing everything alone. Instead of selling an "App," I sold "Peace of Mind." The video ended with the owner relaxing while Heywork-hired staff managed the floor.

The Result

This single video blew up. It drove 250+ signups instantly. Crucially, 90% of the hirers who signed up were from the exact industry (Restaurants) we targeted in the video.

Validation & Product

To validate the market before building, I conducted 130+ interviews with workers and employers. We proved that even with a zero-budget MVP, if the "Trust" problem is solved, the market is ready. We achieved a 90% Completion Rate (90% of posted jobs got filled), proving strong Product-Market Fit.

Birdbox NYC: Selling a $19 Sandwich via "Shock Value"

Orchestrating a viral launch for a premium food brand in a crowded market.

View Works
100% Sell-Out for 3 Months
6,400% IG Growth (400 -> 26k)
$19 Premium Price Point Validated

The Challenge

Birdbox (from San Francisco) was launching in New York City. The Hurdle: They were selling "Claude the Claw"—a fried chicken sandwich with the actual chicken foot and claw still attached. Price: $19 (approx ₹1600). How do you get people to pay premium prices for something that looks terrifying?

Strategy: "Disgust to Desire"

We knew the product was polarizing. Instead of hiding the claw, we made it the hero.

Phase 1: The "WTF" Phase (Awareness)
We flooded social media with high-definition visuals of the Claw. People reacted with shock, disgust, and curiosity. "Is that real?" "Who eats that?" This generated massive organic engagement. The goal was simply: Be Unforgettable.

Phase 2: The "Story" Phase (Conversion)
Once we had attention, we dropped the narrative. The "Claw" wasn't a gimmick; it was proof of Ethical Sourcing. It proved the bird was whole, organic, and head-to-toe fresh—unlike processed nuggets. This flipped the narrative. Eating "Claude" became a flex. It signaled you cared about quality (and were brave enough to try it).

Launch Day Impact

The "Shock + Story" combo worked. We didn't just get customers; we got fans. On launch day, the queue wrapped around the block in NYC. The store sold out completely for 90 days straight.

Wind Chasers: Shifting from "Courses" to "Careers"

Generating 7,000+ leads for a ₹2.5 Lakh Pilot Training Program.

View Works
7,000+ Total Leads Generated
70% Increase in Campus Visits
10+ Immediate Enrollments (₹25L Revenue)

The Challenge

The client was running generic "Enroll Now" ads featuring stock footage of planes. The problem: Every competitor was doing the same. Leads were expensive and low quality because students didn't trust a generic-looking academy with their career.

The "Realism" Pivot

I realized that for a high-stakes decision like Pilot Training, Social Proof is everything.

Strategy 1: The "Hero" Student
We stopped using models. We found a real student who was doing well and filmed a documentary-style ad about his life. No stock footage. Just him, the cockpit, and his dream. This authenticity drastically lowered our Lead Cost.

Strategy 2: The "Founder Story"
The academy was founded because the founder's mother wanted her daughter to be a pilot but couldn't find a good school. We told this origin story. It humanized the brand and built massive trust with parents (the decision-makers).

Growth Tactics

To capture intent, we launched "Open House" events and Webinars. Instead of asking for a ₹2.5L sale online, we asked for a "Campus Visit." This micro-conversion strategy filled the funnel, leading to a sold-out DGCA ground class batch.

11 Point Club: Solving the "I have no one to play with" Problem

Achieving 100% Occupancy for India's Largest Pickleball Facility.

View Works
100% Opening Weekend Occupancy
Zero to Full Launch Velocity

The Challenge

Launching a niche sport (Pickleball) in Bangalore. The Barrier: Pickleball requires 2-4 players. Most people wanted to play but didn't have a team. If we only rented courts to "teams," we would lose 80% of our potential customers (individuals).

The "Group Play" Innovation

We launched a feature called "Group Play" (Join a Game). Instead of booking a court, an individual could pay a small fee to join a "Open Slot." We matched them with strangers.

Why this worked: It solved the "Player Liquidity" problem. Individuals could show up alone and still play. This maximized our court utilization rates during off-peak hours.

Guerrilla Marketing

Since this was a hyper-local business (people won't travel 20km for pickleball), digital ads weren't enough. We hit the streets. We handed out paddles to random pedestrians and challenged them to 60-second games. We filmed these interactions. The content was raw, fun, and local. It created a "FOMO" effect in the neighborhood before we even opened the doors.
